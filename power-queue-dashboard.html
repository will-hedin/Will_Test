Policy & Regulatory Watch

FERC Dec 18, 2025 Co-Location Order (PJM)

FERC issued landmark order directing PJM to develop rules for co-locating data centers at power plants. Creates framework for behind-the-meter generation, three new transmission services, and clarifies interconnection procedures. Enables faster power delivery for DCs willing to co-locate with generation assets. Major shift in regulatory approach.

Date: Dec 18, 2025  ·  Baker Botts

FERC Order 1920 — Transmission Planning Reform

FERC Order 1920 requires grid planners to develop 20-year transmission plans using multiple scenarios, consider grid-enhancing technologies, and give states a robust cost allocation role. RTOs filing compliance plans in 2025–2026. Aimed at proactive transmission expansion to accommodate load growth from data centers, EVs, and industrial customers. Cost allocation rules expected to be contested.

Status: RTO Compliance Filings Ongoing  ·  FERC.gov

FERC ComEd TSAs Approved — March 10, 2026

FERC approved five Transmission Security Agreements between ComEd (Chicago) and data center developers. TSAs include defined ramp schedules, shortfall payments if usage misses commitments, credit obligations, and minimum revenue contributions to protect existing ratepayers from data center cost burden. Model being closely watched as template for other PJM utilities.

Date: March 10, 2026  ·  Utility Dive

Virginia GS-5 Rate Class + 60+ Bills (2026 Session)

SCC approved Dominion's GS-5 rate class in Nov 2025 (effective Jan 2027): data centers >25 MW must pay minimum 85% of contracted distribution/transmission demand and 60% of generation demand. Over 60 data center bills in 2026 VA legislature including HB1515 moratorium (carried to 2027). New Gov. Spanberger pledged data centers must pay "fair share."

Date: Nov 2025 / Jan 2026  ·  DCD

Georgia PSC Approves $16.3B Grid Expansion

Georgia PSC unanimously approved Georgia Power's 10,000 MW capacity expansion in December 2025 — 80% for data centers. Includes 5 new gas plants plus renewables. Total customer cost estimated $50–60B over coming decades including financing and guaranteed utility profit. Southern Environmental Law Center criticized lack of customer protections in the approval.

Date: Dec 19, 2025  ·  Georgia Recorder

Washington Grant PUD Load Caps — March 2025

Grant County PUD implemented unprecedented load caps on data centers in Quincy's industrial zone (March 25, 2025). No formal moratorium but effectively equivalent: no new connections allowed at capacity limits. 79 pending applications; 7–10 year stated wait time. Transmission expansion needed before growth resumes. Solar contract proposed to add supply. WA state debate intensifying over DCs vs. clean energy mandates.

Date: March 25, 2025  ·  QVPR

DOE Directive to FERC — October 23, 2025

US Department of Energy issued formal letter directing FERC to initiate a new rulemaking to "rapidly accelerate the interconnection of large loads." Signals federal-level urgency to streamline data center power delivery as part of broader AI infrastructure policy. FERC subsequently issued the December 2025 co-location order in partial response.

Date: Oct 23, 2025  ·  White & Case

PJM Critical Issue Fast Path (CIFP) — August 2025

PJM Board implemented Critical Issue Fast Path process for large load additions (Aug 8, 2025). New Cycle process launched April 2025 with 1–2 year review timelines (vs. 8+ years historically). PJM also proposing expedited interconnection track for generators supplying large loads. Part of multi-year reform effort to address structural queue bottlenecks.

Date: Aug 8, 2025  ·  PJM.com

CAISO IPE 5.0 Prioritization — June 2025

CAISO launched Interconnection Process Enhancements 5.0 effective June 25, 2025. Shifts from first-come, first-served to readiness-based prioritization (FERC-approved as Track 3). Data centers without early engagement may be deprioritized behind renewables. Aims to reduce the 9.2-year average queue time. Draft Final Proposal published Oct 13, 2025.

Date: June 25, 2025  ·  Zero Emission Grid

Multi-State 2026 Data Center Legislation Wave

Multiple states introduced data center legislation in 2026 tackling energy and tax issues. Virginia (60+ bills), Washington, California, and Georgia are leading. Common themes across bills: utility cost allocation reform, environmental impact reporting requirements, generator use restrictions, siting controls, and new large-load rate structures.
