🕑 Market Notes & Themes Q1 2026

Northern Virginia remains a top hyperscale leasing market with 3 tracked 2026 transactions. Power scarcity is driving deals to adjacent Loudoun and Leesburg.

Emerging markets (Michigan, Indiana, Wisconsin, Oklahoma) are gaining traction as hyperscalers seek available power blocks of 1GW+ unavailable in core markets.

Multi-market deals (Oracle/Digital Realty, Meta Nuclear PPA, DLR $3.25B fund) reflect hyperscalers locking in capacity across portfolios rather than single-site transactions.

AWS pullback: No executed U.S. lease transactions with a 2026 announcement date have been publicly disclosed for AWS as of Q1 2026. Wells Fargo reported AWS paused some international colocation pre-lease discussions in early 2026, but AWS VP Kevin Miller stated demand remains strong.

🏆 Key Macro Themes

$700B+ Total Commitment: Bloomberg reported (Mar 12, 2026) that Microsoft, Meta, Oracle, and Amazon collectively hold $700B+ in future data center lease commitments.

Hyperscaler Capex 2026: $600–625B across the Big 5 (+36% YoY), ~75% tied to AI infrastructure.

Power is the Bottleneck: Lease activity is increasingly driven by power access. Deals announced in Michigan, Wisconsin, Indiana, and Oklahoma reflect utilities' ability to offer 1GW+ blocks unavailable in core markets.

Lease Terms Lengthening: The average disclosed lease term for 2026 deals is 14–15 years, up from 10 years historically, as hyperscalers lock in power-constrained capacity.
