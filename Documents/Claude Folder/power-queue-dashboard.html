⚡ Executive Summary — Top Developments (Q1 2026)

🔴 ERCOT Queue Explodes to 226 GW

ERCOT's large-load queue surged ~300% in 2025, reaching 226 GW by November 2025 (up from 63 GW in Dec 2024). Data centers represent ~73% of all requests. Texas Gov. Abbott signed legislation requiring large loads to fund their share of grid infrastructure. ERCOT and McKinsey are co-developing a framework for short- and mid-term queue solutions, expected in early 2026.

🔴 Dominion Energy: 70 GW Queue vs. 24.7 GW Peak

Dominion Energy Virginia faces a queue nearly 3x its all-time peak demand of 24.7 GW (Jan 2025). ~25 GW has connection dates through 2031; 45 GW remains under study. Dominion filed a new large-load queue management plan with the Virginia SCC in 2026. A new GS-5 rate class for data centers was approved in Nov 2025, requiring developers to directly fund infrastructure costs.

🟡 Georgia PSC Approves 9,885 MW Expansion

On Dec 19, 2025, Georgia regulators unanimously approved Georgia Power's request for 9,885 MW of new generation capacity — the largest grid expansion in state history. 58% gas, 42% battery/solar. The large-load pipeline grew from 16 GW (2023) to 69 GW (Dec 2025), ~80% data centers. PSC imposed cost-allocation rules requiring large-load customers to cover infrastructure expenses.

🟡 FERC Issues Landmark Large-Load Orders

DOE directed FERC on Oct 23, 2025 to issue an ANOPR (Docket RM26-4) with a final-action deadline of Apr 30, 2026. FERC issued a Dec 18, 2025 order requiring PJM to establish co-location framework rules for data centers sharing transmission with generators. Key issues: 60-day study timelines, full cost allocation to large loads, curtailment-in-exchange-for-speed arrangements.

🟢 Moratorium Wave: 12+ States in 2026

At least 12 states introduced data center moratorium bills in 2026: Virginia (until July 2028), New York (3-year), Georgia (1-year from July 2026), Vermont (until July 2030) are among the most advanced. Proposals generally seek time for grid planning, environmental review, and ratepayer protection. None have passed into law as of March 31, 2026.
