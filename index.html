Executive Summary

US utility interconnection queues have reached unprecedented levels driven by AI-fueled data center demand. Power availability has emerged as the single most critical factor in data center site selection, superseding land, fiber, and capital. The following key themes define the 2025–2026 landscape:

⚡
Northern Virginia: Global Epicenter, Maximum Constraint
Dominion carries 70 GW in its queue vs. a 24.7 GW system peak. Wait times reach 5–7 years. The SCC has approved a new GS-5 rate class for 25+ MW customers (effective Jan 2027). Batched queue process capped at 300 MW per request. Data Center Alley expansion is effectively frozen for new entrants without existing queue position.
📈
ERCOT/DFW: Explosive Queue Growth, Faster Process
ERCOT's large-load queue quadrupled to 233 GW in 2025. Oncor alone reports 200 GW. Despite queue size, ERCOT's connect-and-manage structure allows average interconnection in ~1 year for committed projects. New Batch Study process filed with PUC Feb 2026. DFW remains Tier 1 but rapidly tightening.
⛔
Moratoriums & Load Caps Spreading
300+ state bills filed in 30+ states in early 2026. NY, SD, OK pursuing formal construction moratoriums. Grant County PUD (Quincy WA) imposed load-growth caps. At least 63 local moratorium actions introduced or passed. AEP Ohio imposed a de facto moratorium (later lifted with new tariff). Regulatory risk is growing rapidly.
📈
Policy Reform: FERC, ANOPR, and Colocation Rules
DOE directed FERC (Oct 2025) to create new large-load ANOPR. FERC Dec 2025 PJM colocation order creates new transmission services for BTM data centers. FERC Order 2023-A reformed generation interconnection with cluster studies. SPP approved 90-day HILL process. 36+ utilities now have large-load tariffs with deposits of $1–$2.7M/MW.
🏭
Emerging Markets Gaining Share
Columbus OH, Kansas City, Nashville, Reno, Salt Lake City, and Indianapolis are absorbing demand priced out of Tier 1. AEP Ohio's queue fell from 30 GW to 13 GW after tariff reform but 36 sites remain under study. TVA territory demand doubling by 2030. Quincy WA hydropower advantage now impaired by caps. Salt Lake City land values up 8x since 2020.
💰
Hyperscaler Capital Funding Infrastructure
Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Oracle deploying $340B+ in capex in 2025. Hyperscalers signing White House pledge to fund all power delivery infrastructure for their data centers. Oncor's $36B 5-year capital plan driven by data center load. Duke Energy committed $103B. Meta funding $1.2B Entergy transmission for Louisiana campus.

Policy & Regulatory Watch
⚖
FERC Large Load ANOPR — New Rulemaking
October 23, 2025
DOE directed FERC to initiate rulemaking: "Ensuring the Timely and Orderly Interconnection of Large Loads." Targets unprecedented growth from data centers, advanced manufacturing, and large-scale electrification. Expected to reshape how large loads queue for transmission interconnection nationally — the most significant potential federal reform since Order 2023.
⚖
FERC PJM Colocation Order
December 18, 2025
FERC ordered PJM to create rules for co-locating data centers at power plants. Three new transmission service categories for BTM large loads willing to limit grid withdrawals. Follows Feb 2025 show-cause proceeding on tariff fairness. Baker Botts: first major federal framework for BTM data center power. Utilities must develop tariff changes within prescribed timelines.
📈
FERC Order 2023-A — Gen. Interconnection Reform
2024–2025 Implementation
FERC's landmark reform replaced first-come-first-served with cluster study process, reduced speculative applications via stronger financial requirements, and mandated faster study timelines. Implementation ongoing across all ISOs. Aimed at reducing the 2,300+ GW national queue backlog. Over 70% of interconnection applications historically withdrawn — reform aims to reduce this.
⛔
State Moratorium Bills Surging in 2026
Q1 2026 — Ongoing
300+ state data center bills filed across 30+ states in just six weeks of 2026. NY proposing 3-year construction pause while DEC/PSC adopt rules. South Dakota and Oklahoma introducing moratoriums. At least 63 local moratorium actions introduced or adopted across dozens of towns/counties. Environmental groups urging federal pause. Good Jobs First tracking surge in legislative activity.
⛔
Utility Load-Growth Caps & Moratoriums
2025 — Multiple Utilities
Grant County PUD (WA) imposed formal load-growth caps March 2025. AEP Ohio imposed de facto moratorium (lifted July 2025 with new DCT). Dominion informal freeze on new requests over 100 MW without queue position. APS turning away customers due to capacity constraints. At least 5 major utilities have informal or formal new-connection restrictions in place as of Q1 2026.
💰
Large Load Tariffs & Deposit Requirements
2025–2026 — 36+ Utilities
36+ utilities adopted large-load tariffs by late 2025. Common elements: upfront fees, 15-year contracts, 85% minimum monthly bills, security deposits ($1–$2.7M/MW), early-exit fees. Examples: AEP Ohio (85% min-bill), Dominion ($1.5M/MW collateral), Tri-State ($2.7M/MW), ComEd ($1M + $500K/100 MW, ICC-approved March 2026), Virginia ($1.5M/MW collateral requirement).

SPP HILL — 90-Day Interconnection Process
September 4, 2025
SPP Board approved Revision Request 696: targeted 90-day study-and-approval process for High-Impact Large Loads (HILLs). Designed to accelerate data centers, AI facilities, and other power-intensive customers. FERC also approved SPP "provisional load" review process. Most aggressive pro-data-center interconnection reform of any ISO/RTO in 2025. Kansas City and Denver/Aurora are primary beneficiaries.

White House Hyperscaler Power Pledge
2025
Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Oracle signed White House pledge to: build/bring/buy new generation resources AND cover all power delivery infrastructure costs for their data centers (transmission, distribution, grid upgrades). Hyperscalers deploying $340B+ capex in 2025 (50% YoY increase). Shifts grid upgrade costs away from ratepayers for projects covered under the pledge. Meta funding $1.2B Entergy transmission line for Louisiana campus.

PJM 2027 Reliability Shortfall Warning
2025–2026
PJM projects a 6 GW reliability shortfall by 2027 as data center load growth outpaces new generation additions. Capacity auction prices hit price ceiling at $329/MW-day for 2025, locked in for next two years. 32 GW peak load growth forecast through 2030. PJM Large Load Growth Initiative launched to balance reliability with demand. NOVA and Chicago data centers most exposed.

CAISO Electric Rule 30 — Fast-Track Option
2025
CPUC approved PG&E's Electric Rule 30: streamlined pathway for transmission-level retail service. Applicants who fund necessary transmission infrastructure upfront receive accelerated grid connections — potentially years faster than standard queue. Addresses 9.2-year average CAISO queue time. First major mechanism specifically targeting large-load acceleration in California. PG&E 10 GW pipeline to benefit.
📈
AEP Ohio DCT — National Queue Reset Model
July 23, 2025
AEP Ohio Data Center Tariff (DCT) effective July 23, 2025: 85% minimum monthly bill, formal load study required within 45 days ($10K–$100K fee), 25+ MW threshold. Result: queue dropped from 30+ GW to 13 GW as speculative projects withdrew. 36 formal study sites remain. DCT now being watched nationally as model for how tariff reform can rationalize speculative queue applications.
